// the-mark

The Shield.

Our mark is a shield split down the middle: a cloud over a circuit on one side, a cloud over a security lock on the other. Cloud-native on the left, security on the right — the whole chapter in one symbol. White on dark, clean at every size.

CN the official CNSPK shield

What it means

Two halves of one shield. The left carries a cloud feeding into a circuit — cloud-native infrastructure. The right carries a cloud over a lock-and-gear — security, hardened. The seam down the centre is where the two meet: defense in depth.

How to use it

White shield on a dark surface is the default. It ships as brand/assets/cnspk-shield.png (mark only) and cnspk-logo-transparent.png (full lockup with the wordmark). The shared .brand-shield class renders it everywhere — nav, footer, here.

Clear space & surface

Keep clear space around the shield equal to a quarter of its height on every side. Don't recolour it, don't stretch it, and keep it on a dark or photographic background where the white reads cleanly.

// colour-system

Lime on dark. Always.

The palette is dark-mode native: lime accents, charcoal surfaces, bone for warmth. Heritage green and gold are ceremonial only. Every colour below is a token in /css/tokens.css.

primary · lime
Electric Lime #C7FF3E Main accent, CTAs, display highlights. Dark surfaces only — 13.4:1 on Carbon (AAA). --lime
Lime 600 #9BD11A Darker companion. The only lime allowed on bone/light — passes AA at 18px+. --lime-600
Lime Glow #E8FF8A Subtle highlights and hover lift on lime CTAs. --lime-glow
surfaces · neutrals
Carbon #0F1115 Page background. Warmer than pure black. --carbon
Charcoal #1A1D24 Card and panel surfaces. --charcoal
Bone #F4F1EA Primary text on dark; primary surface in light/editorial blocks. 16.4:1 on Carbon (AAA). --bone
heritage · ceremonial only
Pakistan Green #01411C Ceremonial accent — anniversary, state co-brand, gold-paired moments. Used sparingly. --pak-green
Gold #C9A227 Urdu tagline accent at low opacity. Ceremonial only. --gold

The one rule that breaks everything if you ignore it

Electric Lime #C7FF3E scores 1.55:1 on white — a WCAG fail. It only ever appears on dark surfaces. On bone or any light surface, switch to #9BD11A (Lime 600) for text and CTAs.

Lime on Carbon ✓ #C7FF3E on dark · 13.4:1 · AAA
Lime 600 on Bone ✓ #9BD11A on light · AA · never raw #C7FF3E here

// voice

How we sound.

Confident enough to declare, technical enough to be trusted, warm enough to feel like home, fast enough to respect your time.

Lead with the verb, not the apology. Headlines in italic display caps. One Roman Urdu word of attitude per page, where it earns its place — bharosa, mehfil, takleef. CNSPK is its own thing: independent, run by practitioners. CNCF affiliation is a credential, not the headline.

Do
  • Lead with the verb. "Run this command."
  • Declare in italic display caps. "BUILT. TESTED. PAKISTANI."
  • Show the kubectl. Every claim gets a demo, a code block, or a runnable lab. "Here's the five-line fix."
  • One word of attitude, where it lands. "∞ bharosa."
Don't
  • Open with corporate cringe. "Excited to announce… 🚀🔥 buckle up."
  • Apologise before the point. "We'd like to suggest you might consider…"
  • Machine-translate technical terms or publish a full Urdu paragraph. Never translate "Kubernetes" into Urdu.
  • Lead with the CNCF badge. "Pakistan's first CNCF Security Chapter."

// usage

Do's & don'ts.

The mark sits high in the visual hierarchy — large, high-contrast, no effects. Keep it sharp.

Do
  • Put the lime Shield on dark surfaces only.
  • Use #9BD11A (Lime 600) for any lime on light/bone.
  • Keep clear space equal to the CN height on all sides.
  • Scale proportionally and keep high contrast.
  • Set headlines in Bricolage Grotesque — italic, uppercase.
Don't
  • Never place raw #C7FF3E lime on white or bone — it fails WCAG.
  • Don't recolour, stretch, rotate, or add shadows to the mark.
  • Don't use the retired blue palette (#00A2FF / #003366). It's dead.
  • Don't crowd the Shield or set it on busy backgrounds.
  • Don't lead with the CNCF badge as the brand identifier.
