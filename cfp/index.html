// what-we-look-for

Real work beats hot takes.

No vendor pitches. No 40-slide intros to "what is Kubernetes." We want the demo, the incident, the config that actually ran in production.

> Demos over decks

A live cluster, a runnable repo, a terminal you actually type in. Talk is cheap — show the kubectl.

> Pakistani context

SBP guidelines, local fintech scale, the constraints you actually work under. Global topics welcome too — grounded ones land hardest.

> Honest postmortems

The outage, the misconfig, the CVE that shipped. What broke, what you learned, what you'd do again.

> One clear takeaway

The single thing the room can apply Monday morning. If a talk has one, it's a good talk.

// review-timeline

You'll always know where you stand.

No black holes. Here's exactly what happens after you hit submit.

≤ 24 hours

Submission acknowledged

A human confirms we received your proposal. If you don't hear back in a day, ping us — something broke.

≤ 7 days

Committee review

The CFP committee reads every proposal. We may ask a clarifying question or suggest a sharper angle.

≤ 10 days

Decision

Accepted, waitlisted, or a "not this time, here's why." Every submitter gets a real answer — no silent rejections.

// first-time-speakers

Never spoken before? Perfect.

Half of CNSPK's best talks came from someone's first time on a stage. If you've got the substance but not the slides, we pair you with a mentor who's spoken at KubeCon and the chapter before. We help you scope the talk, build the demo, and run a dry-run in front of a friendly room.

You bring the work. We bring the runway. No experience required — just something real you've done with cloud-native security.

Want a mentor first? Note it in your submission, or email hi@cloudnativesecurity.pk.
